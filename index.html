About the foundation

Who Was Esther Idisi?

Esther Idisi dedicated her life to practical compassion. She advocated for abused women, supported orphanages, prepared meals for a leper colony, and cared for street beggars with dignity and tenderness.

The foundation established in her honour continues that same work through community support and a free medical clinic open to families who need care most.

She did not wait to be called. She simply showed up.

Our work

What We Do

Free Medical Clinic

Open weekly, providing consultations, basic medication, and referrals at no cost. The clinic exists so neighbours can seek care before small concerns become emergencies.

Women's Advocacy

Supporting survivors of abuse with counselling, resources, and community networks. We help women find safety, confidence, and trusted people to walk beside them.

Feeding Programmes

Regular meal distribution to the leper colony and street beggars in our community. Every meal is offered with warmth, patience, and respect.

Orphanage Support

Providing supplies, visits, and care packages to local orphanages throughout the year. Our team works to make children feel remembered, protected, and loved.
